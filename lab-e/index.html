The Patagonia

What is Patagonia?

Patagonia is a geographical region that encompasses the southern end of South America, governed by Argentina and Chile. The region comprises the southern section of the Andes Mountains with lakes, fjords, temperate rainforests, and glaciers in the west and deserts, tablelands, and steppes to the east. Patagonia is bounded by the Pacific Ocean on the west, the Atlantic Ocean to the east, and many bodies of water that connect them, such as the Strait of Magellan, the Beagle Channel, and the Drake Passage to the south. The Patagonian Provinces of Argentina are:

  • Tierra del Fuego
  • Santa Cruz
  • Chubut
  • Rio Negro
  • Neuquen
